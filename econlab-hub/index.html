Course Sequence
Ch 1
Introduction to Economics
Scarcity, opportunity cost, micro vs. macro, factors of production, circular flow, and economic systems.
Ch 2
Choice in a World of Scarcity
Budget constraints, opportunity cost, thinking at the margin, PPF, comparative advantage, and positive vs. normative analysis.
Ch 20
International Trade
Absolute vs. comparative advantage, opportunity cost calculations, gains from specialization, and terms of trade.
Ch 3
Demand and Supply
The price mechanism, laws of demand and supply, market equilibrium, curve shifters, four-step analysis, and price controls.
Ch 6
Gross Domestic Product
Measuring the economy: expenditure approach, real vs. nominal GDP, and limitations of GDP as a welfare measure.
Ch 7
Economic Growth
Sources of long-run growth: capital, labor, technology, and the Rule of 70. Includes Ch6 recap.
Ch 8
Unemployment
Who counts, UR & LFPR formulas, U-3 vs U-6, three types, sticky wages, NAIRU, and personal finance.
Ch 9
Inflation
What inflation is, the basket & CPI, three biases, core CPI, historical record, redistribution, indexing, and personal finance.
Ch 11
Aggregate Demand & Supply
Say's vs. Keynes' Law, three curves, AD shifters, output gaps, SRAS zones, demand-pull vs. cost-push, 1973 OPEC stagflation, COVID cases, personal finance dashboard.
Ch 12+13
Keynesian & Neoclassical
Two schools of thought, sticky wages, the multiplier, the Phillips Curve, and policy debates. Includes Ch11 recap.
Ch 14
Money and Banking
Functions of money, M1 vs. M2, bank balance sheets, fractional reserves, and the money multiplier. Includes Ch12+13 recap.
Ch 15
Monetary Policy & Bank Regulation
The Fed, FOMC, policy tools (pre- and post-2008), transmission chain, and the federal funds rate. Includes Ch14 recap.
Ch 17
Government Budgets & Fiscal Policy
Fiscal tools, automatic stabilizers, deficit vs. debt, tax systems, three lags, and crowding out. Includes Ch15 recap.
